Data Exploration: Sales Dataset

Columns Description

  1. ORDERNUMBER: The unique identifier for each order.
  2. QUANTITYORDERED: The number of items ordered.
  3. PRICEEACH: The price of each item.
  4. ORDERLINENUMBER: The line number for the item in the order (useful for orders with multiple items).
  5. SALES: The total sales value (which appears to be the product of QUANTITYORDERED and PRICEEACH for most records).
  6. ORDERDATE: The date when the order was placed.
  7. STATUS: The current status of the order (e.g., "Shipped").
  8. QTR_ID: The quarter of the year in which the order was placed (e.g., 1 for Q1).
  9. MONTH_ID: The month in which the order was placed.
  10. YEAR_ID: The year in which the order was placed.
  11. PRODUCTLINE: The product category or line.
  12. MSRP: Manufacturer's suggested retail price.
  13. PRODUCTCODE: A unique code assigned to each product.
  14. CUSTOMERNAME: The name of the customer who placed the order.
  15. PHONE: The contact phone number for the customer.
  16. ADDRESSLINE1: The first line of the customer's address.
  17. ADDRESSLINE2: The second line of the customer's address (may be empty for many records).
  18. CITY: The city of the customer's address.
  19. STATE: The state of the customer's address.
  20. POSTALCODE: The postal code of the customer's address.
  21. COUNTRY: The country of the customer's address.
  22. TERRITORY: The sales territory or region.
  23. CONTACTLASTNAME: The last name of the contact person for the customer.
  24. CONTACTFIRSTNAME: The first name of the contact person for the customer.
  25. DEALSIZE: A categorical representation of the deal size (e.g., "Small", "Medium").

      Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) Test:

      The ADF test is used to determine the presence of a unit root in the series, and hence helps in understanding if the series is stationary or not.

      The p-value is 0.0044, which is less than the typical threshold of 0.05, suggesting that the time series is stationary.

      The test statistic is less than the critical values at 1%, 5%, and 10% confidence levels, further indicating stationarity.

      Given that the series is already stationary, we don't need to apply further transformations (like differencing or logarithmic transformation) for now.

    This suggests that the first-order auto-regressive (AR) and moving average (MA) terms in the SARIMAX model are not statistically meaningful.

    Autocorrelation at lag 1 is not statistically significant in the residuals of the model, indicating that there is no strong linear dependence between adjacent observations.

    Residuals might not be perfectly normally distributed, but they're not significantly non-normal based on the Jarque-Bera test.

    The Jarque-Bera test suggests that while the residuals may not follow a perfect normal distribution, they do not deviate significantly from normality.

    There's evidence of heteroskedasticity in the residuals, indicating the model might not capture all patterns in the data.

    The presence of heteroskedasticity in the residuals suggests that the model may not adequately capture all patterns and variations in the data.
